LEGAL RED FLAG PATTERNS

CRITICAL RISK:
- Confidentiality expires automatically without survival clause.
- Residuals clause allowing memory-based use without restriction.
- Unlimited indemnification without liability cap.
- No limitation of liability clause.
- Governing law unrelated to both parties without justification.
- No exclusion clause for public information.

HIGH RISK:
- Confidentiality term less than 2 years.
- One-sided indemnity.
- No return/destruction requirement.
- No purpose limitation.
- No remedy or injunctive relief provision.

MEDIUM RISK:
- Vague definition of confidential information.
- Undefined “reasonable efforts.”
- Ambiguous jurisdiction wording.
- Overly broad definition without exclusions.

LOW RISK:
- Formatting inconsistencies.
- Minor grammatical errors.

CRITICAL ESCALATION RULES:

- If confidentiality obligations cease upon termination → CRITICAL.
- If indemnity is unlimited and no liability cap exists → CRITICAL.
- If residuals clause allows unrestricted use → HIGH or CRITICAL.
- If governing law is offshore without operational connection → HIGH.
