NDA BEST PRACTICES – RISK INTELLIGENCE NOTES

CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION DEFINITION:
- Should not be overly broad or vague.
- Should clearly define scope.
- Should avoid including casual oral statements unless documented.
- Should define marking requirements or confirmation process.

EXCLUSIONS:
- Must clearly state exclusions.
- Absence of exclusions creates enforceability ambiguity.

TERM:
- Agreement term and confidentiality survival must be separate.
- Trade secrets should survive indefinitely.
- Fixed short survival (<2 years) weakens protection.

PURPOSE LIMITATION:
- Should clearly restrict use to defined purpose.
- Broad purpose definitions weaken enforceability.

REMEDIES:
- Should allow injunctive relief.
- Absence of remedy clause reduces enforcement leverage.

DATA SECURITY:
- Should specify standard of care (reasonable care or industry standard).
- No protection standard clause is a weakness.

MUTUALITY:
- One-sided obligations increase negotiation risk.
